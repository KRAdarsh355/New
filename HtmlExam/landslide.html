Landslides in Wayanad

A recent landslide struck the Kalladi tunnel construction site near Meppadi in Wayanad, Kerala, on 7 July 2026, following days of intense monsoon rainfall. The landslide buried construction equipment and workers, disrupted rescue operations, and caused significant damage to the project site. After several days of search efforts by the NDRF, Fire and Rescue Services, police, and local volunteers, the death toll rose to eight, with multiple workers injured. The incident has renewed concerns about the safety of infrastructure projects in the ecologically fragile Western Ghats and prompted investigations into whether construction practices and debris dumping contributed to the disaster.



Rescue teams recovered the final missing worker from the Wayanad tunnel landslide. This recovery brings the total death toll from the incident to eight people. The six-day search operation concluded after the last body was found. Vikram Rana, a construction manager, was identified as the last deceased victim. Efforts to clear debris and restore the affected stretch will commence shortly.

Personnel from the National Disaster Response Force (NDRF) led the search operation. Three bodies were recovered on the first day, while the remaining victims were found over the following days. Rana's body was taken to Vythiri Taluk Hospital for a post-mortem examination before being shifted to Kozhikode Government Medical College Hospital for embalming ahead of its transportation to his native place, officials said.
